Design Reflection

My design system reflects my personal style and identity through color, typography, and spacing choices. I selected a color palette that feels calm and balanced, combining shades of green, blue, and purple. The typography uses a clean and modern sans-serif font that feels approachable while still professional. Using different heading sizes and body text allows me to create a clear hierarchy and makes content easy to read.

Accessibility was an important factor in designing this system. I tested color combinations for sufficient contrast to ensure readability for all users. I also added clear styles for buttons and links so people navigating with keyboards can easily see where they are on the page. One challenge was balancing colors that I personally liked with ones that were more accessible. I found that adjustments to saturation and brightness allowed me to keep my preferred aesthetic.

Creating a cohesive design system helped me understand how different visual elements work together. I was surprised at how small changes in spacing or color can affect the overall feel of a page. Defining a consistent spacing scale and font sizes makes it easier to build pages that look balanced and professional. I can apply this system to future projects, including my portfolio, to maintain a consistent visual identity across different pages and components.

In the future, I might expand this system by adding more interactive components to cover more types of content. I may also explore different color palettes for different contexts while keeping the typography and spacing consistent. Overall, this system provides a good foundation for designing interfaces that are both visually appealing and user friendly. It is flexible enough to adapt to various projects while maintaining a consistent style.
